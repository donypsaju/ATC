The Crisis in PWD Reservation for Kerala Aided Schools

The implementation of the PWD Act in Kerala's aided schools is facing critical failures. While intended to provide reservation, the process is stalled, creating a chain reaction that harms candidates, stalls appointments, and ultimately violates students' Right to Education. This page outlines the core issues based on public data.

Issue 01: Drastic Mismatch in Supply vs. Demand

The Problem: There is a fundamental mismatch between the number of reserved vacancies and the number of qualified PWD candidates available in employment exchanges.

The chart below compares the total posts to be filled (Demand, from Samanwaya) with the total available candidates (Supply, from RTI data). The data clearly shows there are not enough qualified candidates to fill the backlog.

Issue 04: Violation of Students' Right to Education

The Final Impact: The direct consequence of thousands of unapproved and vacant posts is a severe shortage of adequate staff in schools.

This is not just an administrative problem; it is a direct violation of the Right to Education (RTE) Act. Students are being denied qualified teachers because the administrative process for reservation is broken. This backlog must be cleared to protect the rights of students.
